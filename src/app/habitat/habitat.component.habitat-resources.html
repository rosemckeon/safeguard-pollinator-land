Habitat resources

The various components (other than floral resources) within an ecosystem that provide essential elements for the survival, reproduction, and overall health of organisms within that habitat. This includes access to fresh water, nest sites (cavities in vegetation or soil, aquatic larval habitats) and alternative (non-floral) resources (e.g., insect prey for larvae of hoverfly (Syrphidae) or Empidae species, dung for certain fly species (e.g. Muscidae), larval host plants for Lepidoptera).
